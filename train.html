IMPORTANT FORMULAE AND CONCEPTS

  1. Relative speed of train or objects moving in same direction is difference between the speed of objects/trains
  2. Relative speed of train or objects moving in opposite direction is sum of the speed of objects/trains
  3. Distance covered when train crosses platform/bridge is sum of length of bridge/platform and train
  4. Distance covered when train crosses pole/man is length of the train

  1. A 250 m long train is running at the speed of 40 km/hr. Find the time taken to cross a pole at the station
    1. 20 s
    2. 22.5 s
    3. 25 s
    4. 30.5 s

    Speed = 40*(5/18) = 11.111 m/s
    Distance = 250 m
    Time = distance/speed = 250/11.11 = 22.5 s

  2. A 160 m long train crosses a 160 m long platform in 16 seconds. Find the speed of the train
    1. 20 m/s
    2. 25 m/s
    3. 70 km/hr
    4. 30 m/s

    Distance covered = 160+160 = 320 m
    Speed = 320/16 = 20 m/s
