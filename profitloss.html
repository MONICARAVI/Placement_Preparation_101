IMPORTANT FORMULAE AND CONCEPTS

  1. The amount at which an item is purchased is called cost price
  2. The amount at which an item is sold is called selling price
  3. If selling price is greater than cost price then the difference is called gain or profit
  4. If cost price is greater than selling price then the difference is called loss
  5. Discount is given on the marked price
  6. Loss% = (loss/cp)*100
  7. Gain% = (gain/cp)*100

  1. Mr. X purchased an AC for $12000 and sold it for $15000. What was the profit?
    1. $3000
    2. $300
    3. $3300
    4. $330

    Cost price = 12000
    selling price = 15000
    Profit = SP-CP = 15000-12000 = 3000

  2. A gold ring is sold for Rs.14500 at a loss of 20% what is the cost price of gold ring?
    1. Rs. 18125
    2. Rs. 16800
    3. Rs. 17400
    4. Rs. 15225

    SP = Rs. 14500
    Loss% = 20
    80%(CP) = SP
    (80/100)*(CP) = 14500
    CP = 18125
